Your Rising Sign (Ascendant)

What's a Rising Sign, Anyhow?

In astrology, your rising sign is the portal through which you perceive the external world. Your rising sign, or ascendant, governs elements of your appearance as well as the manifestation of your outward personality. It is also the first impression you give to others. Think of it as your facade, the natural mask to which you default when you're with unfamiliar people, in unfamiliar places and situations. When you walk into a room, you are radiating the qualities of your rising sign. If you are a Leo sun with a Scorpio rising, then although you are a Leo at heart and people will get to know that lion or lioness within, when people meet you for the first time, they meet a Scorpio. The presentation of the personality, as determined by the ascendant, always precedes the presentation of one's true essence -- what one is at one's core, what is at the center of your being.

Your rising sign is determined by your birth date, location, and your accurate birth time, as it changes zodiac signs approximately every two hours. Technically speaking, it's the zodiac sign that was rising or ascending on the eastern horizon the moment you were born. The rising sign sets up the pie-shaped structure of the 12 houses in your birth chart and informs your chart ruler.

Essentially, your rising sign is your public persona, the "hat" or "costume" you wear when people first meet you. It is how you initially appear to others, the first impression that you make. In some ways, it is a facade, for it is not who we truly are deep within ourselves, at our core -- the nature of our real, true essence is described by our Sun sign -- but rather, the ascendant sign describes how we present ourselves to the external world and how we wish to be seen.
