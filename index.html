Welcome to What's Your Sign?!

A quick reference guide for people interested in learning about astrology

Your personal astrological profile is totally unique. No one else has a chart identical to yours, unless they were born on the same date, in the same place, and at the exact same time as you! (In that case, you would have an astrological twin.)

This is an introductory overview of the 12 zodiac signs, interpreted through the Sun, Moon, and Rising Sign (Ascendant) positions in a natal (birth) chart.

Astrology is a fascinating study of the interplay between the celestial sphere and the material world. The information you will find here is meant to be a brief introduction to a vast and complex subject that can take many years to properly understand.

On this website, you will get a glimpse of the three most significant factors in a natal chart, plus links to additional resources for further information. Most people already know their Sun sign, but you might need to click on the links to obtain your Moon and Ascendant signs, if you do not already know them.

The resources provided by the links offer in-depth analysis via personalized reports for a nominal charge, but you can create a profile and obtain basic information like your Moon and Rising signs, plus other planets and their house positions in your own natal chart, free of charge, at Astrodienst.com and other listed websites (please see "Resources" for the links).

While some people elect to use astrology in a predictive fashion, I have always found its most useful application to be in the context of understanding the personality profiles of individuals with whom I regularly interact. That is why I always obtain the birth information (day, month, year, time, and place of birth) from every person who stands to play a key role in my world. Whether friend or foe, classmate or colleague, lunch buddy or lover, if you're in my life, then I am going to do the research and construct your complete astrological profile.

I do this so that I can have a solid grasp on exactly what makes you tick. If I understand the various complex factors simmering beneath the surface of a person's well-constructed facade, then it is likely I will most often have the advantageous upper hand in that interpersonal relationship. It is not a foolproof strategy, but it sure beats blind guessing when it comes to predicting how someone is likely to respond to a tricky situation or in a particular set of complicated circumstances. The insights have always proven useful.
